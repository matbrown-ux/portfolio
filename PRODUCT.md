# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Founders and business owners evaluating a UX/UI engineer, SEO specialist, or agentic workflow developer for a significant project. They arrive comparison-shopping, carry real budget, and are deciding whether Mathew is worth the investment. Their context: pressed for time, high standards, low tolerance for ambiguity. The job is not to browse — it's to reach a fast, confident hiring decision.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that converts high-value freelance clients across three service areas: UX/UI Engineering, SEO, and Agentic Workflow Development. Success looks like a potential client arriving, feeling immediately certain they've found a credible expert, and initiating contact with a real project. The site must do the selling — Mathew shouldn't have to.

## Brand Personality

Credible, premium, decisive. The emotional target: clients feel excited to start the project and comfortable committing a high budget. Not "impressive portfolio" energy — "I found the right person" energy.

## Anti-references

- Generic developer portfolio templates (hero + skill bars + project grid)
- SaaS marketing cream aesthetic (Inter, white backgrounds, teal accents, identical feature cards)
- Dribbble-style portfolio bravado — loud, performative, over-designed
- Startup hustle energy — busy, social-proof-stacked, conversion-optimized in an obvious way
- Agency sites that prioritize awards over clarity

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The portfolio is itself a UX/UI deliverable. Every layout decision, typographic choice, and interaction is a live demonstration of the work on offer. There is no separation between showing and telling.
2. **Premium reads as restraint.** High-value clients interpret discipline as confidence. Over-designed means trying too hard. The site should feel like it doesn't need to impress anyone — and that confidence is what impresses.
3. **Expertise through specificity.** Vague generalism loses premium clients. Every claim is precise: specific outcomes, specific services, specific evidence. Clarity is the differentiator.
4. **Range without dilution.** Three service areas must feel unified under one clear point of view, not scattered across three unrelated pitches. The through-line is high-craft, high-judgment work.
5. **The site closes the deal.** This is not a gallery. It is a business development tool. Every page answers the question a client has before they know to ask it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Sufficient color contrast on all text. Keyboard navigable.
